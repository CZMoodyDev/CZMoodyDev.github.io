Youth Adventure Camps: Ages 8 to 12

Willow Creek Resident Site

These camps are located at our Willow Creek site in the foothills of the beautiful Livingstone Range. They will learn age relevant teaching from the Bible around the campfire in the evenings and be challenged in God's Creation during the day. With staff to camper ratios of 3:1 and a warm place to come home to at night, they can experience the thrill of the land with comfort and confidence.

Facilities include cabins, washhouse with showers, toilets and sinks, food cooked in our kitchen and a couple of tarped areas where we eat outside.

Our 8-12 year old camps are a great way to introduce your young children to the outdoors and God's Creation. With activities planned for a younger age group, your children will experience the wilderness in a safe yet engaging environment. Led by trained staff, your child is bound to be challenged and engaged for their adventure.

Our Willow Creek site has cabins, washrooms, and a fully equipped kitchen.

  • Canoeing/Kayaking
  • Rock Climbing
  • Rappelling
  • Campfire Cooking
  • Hiking
  • Horseback Riding (3 hours during the week)
  • Field Games
  • Campfires
  • Bible Devotions

Coat Creek Wilderness Site

This remote site has enough amenities to be comfortable, however, we leave the luxuries behind. They will learn age relevant teaching from the Bible around the campfire in the evenings and be challenged in God's Creation during the day. With staff to camper ratios of 3:1 our staff will guide your pre-teens through a challenging and thrilling journey.

These camps are designed to motivate this group of maturing pre-teens through trial and thrill. With activities planned for an active and developing age group, your children will experience adventure and challenge in a safe and engaging approach. Led by trained staff, your child will be guided in developing leadership and outdoor skills.

You can expect your children to build relationships, and to start building competence in the outdoors and leadership through activities such as:

  • Hiking/Scrambling
  • Horseback Riding (3 hours during the week)
  • Wilderness Skills
  • Campfire Cooking
  • Fishing
  • Field Games
  • Campfires
  • Bible Devotions
